# Product — Paladin (Expanded Copy)

## Page hero
**H1:** Paladin
**Sub:** Real-time counter-intelligence for recruiting calls. It listens to both
sides of the conversation and turns it into live, on-screen intelligence — so you
lead the call instead of scrambling to keep up.

## The Intelligence Pane
**Lead:** Everything Paladin surfaces appears in one calm, glanceable panel beside
your call — never in your ear, never interrupting your flow.

- **Live transcript** of both sides, lightly annotated
- **Prompt feed** — suggested questions, timed to the conversation
- **Context cards** — definitions for technical terms as they come up
- **Signal markers** — moments worth revisiting flagged in real time

## Feature deep-dives

### 1. Pre-Call Skills Gap Analysis
Feed Paladin three things — a LinkedIn URL, the résumé, and the job description —
and it produces a structured skills-gap read before you dial: where the candidate
likely maps to the role, where the résumé is thin, and the exact areas worth
probing. You start the call with a plan, not a cold read.

### 2. Live Prompts
As the conversation unfolds, Paladin pushes targeted, context-aware follow-up
questions to your screen. Miss nothing when a candidate glosses over a gap; go
deeper exactly when it counts. You stay in control — the prompts are a co-pilot,
not an autopilot.

### 3. Real-Time Context
When a candidate drops a framework, tool, or acronym you don't know, Paladin
defines it on-screen in plain language, instantly. Interview senior candidates in
unfamiliar domains with confidence — and ask the sharper second question because
you understood the first answer.

### 4. Post-Call Summary
Seconds after you hang up, Paladin delivers a client-ready candidate summary:
key takeaways, a skills-match confidence score, and a culture-fit read. Send it
straight to your hiring manager or client — no scramble to reconstruct the call
from memory and scribbled notes.

## Why "no app required" matters
Paladin runs through your existing phone. There's nothing for you or the candidate
to download, no new dialer to learn, no friction on either end. It rings you,
you pick up, it connects the candidate. That's it.

## Differentiation (full)
Recruiting tech clusters at two ends of the timeline: tools that screen people out
*before* a conversation (ATS, résumé parsers) and tools that review it *after*
(recorded-interview platforms). A third category tries to remove the human
entirely (chatbots, async AI screens).

Paladin is built for the middle — the live conversation — because that's where
judgment actually happens. It doesn't replace the recruiter's instinct; it arms it
with better information, in real time.
